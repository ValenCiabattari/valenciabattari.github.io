Numerical Study of the Blasius Boundary Layer

Academic project focused on the numerical solution of the Blasius boundary layer equation, including characteristic thickness evaluation, velocity-field analysis, vorticity estimation, and circulation calculation.

Academic Project | Fluid Mechanics | 2023

Overview

This academic project addressed the classical laminar boundary layer problem introduced by Blasius. The work combined fluid mechanics theory with numerical implementation in MATLAB in order to solve the nonlinear ordinary differential equation associated with the self-similar boundary layer formulation.

Scope of Work

The project began with the theoretical development of the boundary layer model, including the no-slip condition, characteristic thickness definitions, vorticity, circulation, and the self-similar transformation leading to the Blasius equation.

A numerical algorithm was then implemented to solve the problem using an iterative shooting method together with MATLAB ODE solvers. Once the solution was obtained, the resulting velocity profile was used to evaluate characteristic boundary layer quantities and additional flow properties.

Numerical Method and Results

  • Implemented the Blasius equation solution in MATLAB using ODE45.
  • Applied an iterative shooting method to satisfy the asymptotic boundary condition.
  • Obtained the normalized velocity profile and identified the boundary layer thickness criterion.
  • Calculated boundary layer thickness, displacement thickness, and momentum thickness.
  • Computed local velocity components for arbitrary x and y positions inside the boundary layer.
  • Estimated vorticity and circulation from the numerical solution.

Technical Focus

One of the most valuable aspects of the project was connecting the mathematical formulation of the Blasius problem with its physical interpretation. The work made it possible to move from the abstract nonlinear differential equation to meaningful aerodynamic quantities such as characteristic thicknesses, velocity distribution, and rotational flow behavior inside the boundary layer.

Tools and Deliverables

MATLAB was used for the numerical implementation, iterative solution procedure, tabulated results, and graph generation. The final deliverable was a complete technical report documenting the methodology, governing equations, numerical approach, obtained results, and engineering conclusions.
