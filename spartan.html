Flight Performance and Stability Analysis of the Alenia C-27J Spartan

Academic project developed within the Flight Mechanics course, focused on the evaluation of aircraft performance together with static and dynamic stability analysis of the Alenia C-27J Spartan.

Academic Project | Flight Mechanics | 2024

Overview

This academic project involved a comprehensive study of the Alenia C-27J Spartan from a flight mechanics perspective. The work covered aircraft performance, static stability, and dynamic stability, combining theoretical analysis, computational implementation, and technical interpretation of the results.

Scope of Work

The first part of the project focused on aircraft performance. This included steady, straight and level flight, climbing flight, rate and angle of climb, service ceiling, maneuvering flight, range and endurance, takeoff, landing, and the corresponding operational and regulatory considerations.

The second part addressed static stability, including longitudinal, directional, and lateral stability. The analysis involved trim-related behavior, control effectiveness, minimum control speed, maximum crosswind considerations, rudder lock tendencies, pedal forces, neutral point assessment, and the influence of different control and power assumptions on stability limits.

The final part focused on dynamic stability. Longitudinal and lateral-directional stability derivatives were obtained, the main aircraft modes were identified, and the temporal response of the phugoid, short-period, Dutch roll, roll, and spiral modes was evaluated. Reduced-order models were also studied for comparison and validation purposes.

Key Results

  • Built and interpreted the aircraft flight envelope.
  • Obtained relevant performance metrics such as rate of climb and service ceiling.
  • Analyzed trim and static stability behavior through characteristic aerodynamic and control curves.
  • Evaluated stability limits and control-related parameters under different operating assumptions.
  • Identified and studied the main dynamic modes of the aircraft through time-response analysis.
  • Compared complete formulations with reduced-order dynamic models.

Technical Focus

One of the most valuable aspects of the project was the integration of multiple flight mechanics topics into a single coherent analysis framework. Rather than studying performance and stability separately, the project helped connect operational aircraft behavior, stability margins, control authority, and modal response into a broader engineering understanding of the aircraft.

Tools and Deliverables

Python was used for calculations and result visualization, while LaTeX was used to prepare the final technical report. The project was documented as a complete academic PDF report including methodology, numerical results, and graphical outputs.
